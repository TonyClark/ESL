Papers and Reports

The motivation for ESL is to produce an executable modelling environment that supports simulations of organizations. The origins of this work started around 2010 with the development of a modelling language called LEAP that was based on a variation of UML. LEAP developed into a collection of techniques and an associated tool-set that was used to model, simulate and analyze organizations and various organizational use-cases. The core of the LEAP tool-set was a component-based simulation language based on events. Concurrently, we started to develop a vision for the Model Driven Organization (MDO) and how decision-making could be supported by allowing organizational goals to be checked against simulation runs. Analysis of the technology requirements for these research challenges led us to propose that executable organizational models for organizational analysis and decision-making can be supported by an actor-language that has produced ESL.
